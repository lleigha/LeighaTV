<p>
Leigha Dennis is a senior design director at 2x4, where she leads experience and spatial design projects that integrate architecture, branding, and storytelling. Her recent clients include the Princeton University Art Museum, Fubon Art Museum, the New Museum, Dia: Chelsea, Tiffany & Co., Hyundai, and Intuit. Leigha previously held administrative and faculty appointments at Columbia University’s Graduate School of Architecture, Planning and Preservation, served as a Muschenheim Fellow at the University of Michigan’s Taubman College, and was a fellow at the Akademie Schloss Solitude in Stuttgart. She holds degrees in architecture from the University of Michigan and Columbia University.
</p>
<p>
Leigha’s art practice examines the impact of digital technologies and network culture on architecture and the built environment. Working often with documentary forms, she designs environments, objects, and media. Her work has been published and exhibited at notable institutions including the New Museum, the Museum of Modern Art, the Museum of the City of New York, and the Contemporary Art Centre in Vilnius.
</p>
