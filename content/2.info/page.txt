Leigha Dennis is a designer and researcher in Brooklyn, New York, where she investigates the convergence of architecture and digital culture. She is an adjunct faculty member at Columbia University's <a target="_blank" href="http://www.arch.columbia.edu">Graduate School of Architecture, Preservation and Planning</a>, where she co-directs the Architecture Online Lab and is a member of the <a target="_blank" href="http://www.networkarchitecturelab.org/">Network Architecture Lab</a>. She was recently the 2013-14 Muschenheim Fellow at the University of Michiganâ€™s <a target="_blank" href="http://taubmancollege.umich.edu/">Taubman College of Architecture and Urban Planning</a>.  

She received a Master of Architecture from Columbia University, and a B.S. in Architecture from the University of Michigan. She has worked as an architect, designer and researcher in San Francisco and New York, and has established a number of collaborations, among them with the Buell Center for American Architecture, MOS, and AUDC. Her speculative design, research, and writing have been published in New Geographies, Bracket, The New City Reader, Junk Jet, Domus, The Buell Hypothesis and has been exhibited at the New Museum, the Museum of Modern Art, MoMA PS1, the Museum of the City of New York and the Van Alen Institute.
